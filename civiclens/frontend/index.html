    What bad AQI does to you

    • PM2.5 penetrates deep into lungs and bloodstream, raising risk of asthma, bronchitis and heart disease.
    • Children, the elderly and pregnant women face disproportionately higher risk even at "Moderate" levels.
    • Long-term exposure is linked to reduced lung development in children and lower life expectancy.

    What you can do today

    • Avoid outdoor exercise when AQI is above 150; prefer early morning hours.
    • Use N95 masks on high-AQI days; run air purifiers indoors if available.
    • Report visible burning, dust sources or smoke-emitting vehicles right here — every report helps build the evidence trail.
    • Carpool, use public transport, or switch to EVs to cut your own contribution.
